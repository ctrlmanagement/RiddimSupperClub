01 — ARCHITECTURE

One Domain.
Three Audience States. ✓ Locked

The RIDDIM platform operates on a single domain with three distinct UI environments — each calibrated to its audience's context, lighting conditions, and emotional state. The public site courts strangers into desire. The member portal converts desire into loyalty. The staff dashboard turns loyalty into operational execution.

🌐
Environment 01
Public Site
  • Home — cinematic hero, patio showcase
  • Menu — visual/interactive scroll
  • Reservations — API-connected form
  • Gallery — lazy-load, filterable
  • Events — calendar + slug pages
  • Private Events — conversion page
  • Join — member registration CTA
👑
Environment 02
Member Portal
  • Dashboard — points, tier, wallet
  • Secret Menu — members-only access
  • QR Wallet — digital membership card
  • Benefits — tonight's active perks
  • Reservations — member request flow
  • Visit History — gamified timeline
  • Concierge — SMS thread UI
⚡
Environment 03
Staff Dashboard
  • Guest lookup + VIP notes
  • Reward redemption toggle
  • Floor plan status (live seats)
  • Check-in via QR scan
  • Role-scoped SMS threads
  • Reservation queue
  • Owner analytics + audit log

Staff UI — No Animation Policy

Staff interfaces operate in fast-paced, dimly lit, high-stakes environments. All state changes are instant. The only motion allowed is a 150ms color-state transition on status chips (waiting → seated → done). No page transitions, no entrance animations, no loading theatrics. Speed is the UX.

06 — MEMBER RECOGNITION

The Moment of
Recognition. ⚠ Partial

The four-beat sequence that makes a returning member feel seen from the first pixel. This is not generic personalization — it is a choreographed identity confirmation ceremony.

01
Name Drop
First name appears first — before tier, before points. Large, serif, italic. "Welcome back, Marcus." Not a greeting: a recognition.
02
Tier Signal
Tier badge materializes with a color flash. Gold for Obsidian, silver for Standard. The visual hierarchy tells them where they stand without words.
03
Points Count-Up
Animated counter from 0 to current balance. Turns each visit into a visible reward. If they earned points last visit, the delta highlights briefly in gold.
04
Tonight's Perks
Active perks for this evening stacked below — what they can use right now, not later. Immediacy over abstraction. The card knows what night it is.

08 — REFERENCE SITE ANALYSIS

What the
Benchmarks Taught Us. ✓ Reference Only

Bekeb (bekebsma.com) — Artisanal Cocktail Bar

What they do right: Full-bleed photography as the dominant design element. Navigation is invisible until needed. The brand story is embedded in the imagery, not the copy. The site feels like looking through a window at something you want to be inside.

What RIDDIM takes: Lead with photography. The outdoor patio is RIDDIM's strongest differentiator — it should open the homepage exactly the way Bekeb opens theirs: full screen, no chrome, let the venue speak first.

Drai's Supper Club (draisnyc.com) — Modern Supper Club

What they do right: The "Downstairs at Drai's" dual-environment concept maps directly to RIDDIM's dining + nightclub identity. Clear navigation hierarchy. Live jazz + fine dining combination is clearly communicated. The multi-space narrative gives the venue depth and multiple reasons to return.

What RIDDIM takes: Name each experience. "The Patio," "The Dining Room," "The Floor" — named spaces with their own visual identity create a sense of place that drives return visits. Reservation widget is prominent and persistent across the site.

Sip & Guzzle (sipandguzzlenyc.com) — Members Club Bar

What they do right: Dual-venue model (Sip + Guzzle) clearly communicated. Hours prominently displayed. Merch as community signal. The brand feels like a local secret even though it's a NYC venue — that insider warmth is exactly what a members club needs.

What RIDDIM takes: The members portal should feel like a secret. Not just gated access — but a different visual register entirely. The contrast between the public site's "invite you in" energy and the portal's "you belong here" familiarity is a deliberate design move.

The RIDDIM Competitive Gap

None of these three reference sites have a native members club with point tracking, a digital wallet, or staff-facing operations infrastructure. That triple-stack — public site + member portal + staff dashboard — on a single domain, at this visual quality level, does not exist in the Atlanta market. This is the moat.
